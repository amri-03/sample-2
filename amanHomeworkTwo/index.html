Springtime in Michigan

Springtime in Ann Arbor is a beautiful time. Even though it can be chilly at times, it is nice to walk my dog Bacon. This site includes some pictures I have taken on these walks.


Sometimes we walk around the neighborhood, other times we make it to the woods. Ann Arbor also has some beautiful lakes and rivers including the Huron River Watertrail and the Argo Park Cascades.


Spring also brings with it a sense of renewal that can be felt all across the city. The university campus comes alive with students enjoying the warmer weather, reading on the lawns, or gathering in coffee shops with open windows. Local farmers’ markets begin to reappear, filling the air with the smells of fresh bread, flowers, and seasonal produce. Downtown Ann Arbor feels especially vibrant this time of year, with street performers returning to the sidewalks and restaurants setting up patios for outdoor dining. The blend of city life with the changing season makes even a short walk into town feel like an adventure, and it’s easy to find new details to appreciate every day.

On days when the sun is shining, there are also many parks and trails just a short drive away that offer beautiful spaces to explore. Gallup Park, with its peaceful walking paths and views of the water, is a favorite spot for many locals in the spring. The Matthaei Botanical Gardens and Nichols Arboretum are also stunning this time of year, with colorful flowers and quiet trails perfect for wandering. Even simple routines, like walking a dog or taking a break from a busy day, become opportunities to enjoy the beauty of nature that surrounds Ann Arbor. Spring is never predictable here—it may rain one day and be warm the next—but that variety is part of the charm, making each outing feel unique and memorable.
What makes spring in Ann Arbor so enjoyable is the way the season seems to invite people outdoors. Families ride bikes along the trails, joggers take advantage of the crisp air, and groups gather for picnics in open green spaces. The city feels connected by these shared moments, whether it’s pausing to admire the blooming magnolia trees or watching the sun set over the river. Even on cooler days, there’s an energy in the air that makes it worthwhile to step outside. With every walk, every bloom, and every breeze, spring offers a reminder of why Ann Arbor is such a special place to call home.
